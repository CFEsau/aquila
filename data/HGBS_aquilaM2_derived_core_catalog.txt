| "First-generation" product from the Herschel Gould Belt survey (HGBS - André et al. 2010, A&A, 518, L102)
|
| Published in Könyves et al. (2015 - arXiv:1507.05926): "A census of dense cores in the Aquila cloud complex: SPIRE/PACS observations from the Herschel Gould Belt survey"

| Acknowledgements: If you use this HGBS product for a publication, please refer to the above two papers (Könyves et al. 2015 and André et al. 2010).
|
| Description of columns:
|
|  (1)   Core running number.
|  (2)   Core name. The full name consists of the HGBS_J prefix followed by a tag created from the J2000 sexagesimal coordinates.
|  (3)   Core right ascension (J2000).
|  (4)   Core declination (J2000).
|  (5)   Geometrical average between the major and minor FWHM sizes of the core [pc], as measured in the high-resolution column density map after deconvolution from the 18.2" HPBW resolution of the map.
|  (6)   Geometrical average between the major and minor FWHM sizes of the core [pc], as measured in the high-resolution column density map before deconvolution from the 18.2" HPBW resolution of the map. 
|  (7)   Estimated core mass [Msun] assuming the dust opacity law advocated by Roy et al. (2014, A&A, 562, A138).
|  (8)   Uncertainty in the core mass [Msun], including calibration uncertainties, but excluding dust opacity uncertainties.
|  (9)   Dust temperature [K] derived from simple SED fitting of the core integrated flux densities between 160 micron and 500 micron.
| (10)   Uncertainty in the dust temperature [K], including calibration uncertainties, but excluding uncertainties in the dust opacity index.
| (11)   Peak H2 column density [10^21 cm^-2] at the resolution of the 500 micron data, derived from a graybody SED fit to the core peak flux densities in a common 36.3" beam at all wavelengths. 
| (12)   Average column density of the core [10^21 cm^-2] derived using the observed radius (col. 6).
| (13)   Average column density of the core [10^21 cm^-2] derived using the deconvolved radius (col. 5).
| (14)   Beam-averaged peak volume density [10^4 cm^-3] at the resolution of the 500 micron data, derived from the peak column density (col. 11) assuming a Gaussian spherical distribution.
| (15)   Average volume density of the core [10^4 cm^-3] derived using the observed radius (col. 6).
| (16)   Average volume density of the core [10^4 cm^-3] derived using the deconvolved radius (col. 5).
| (17)   Bonnor-Ebert mass ratio of the core, M_BE,crit/Mobs.
| (18)   Core type: starless, prestellar, protostellar.
| (19)   Comments: "no SED fit", "tentative bound", or "CO high-V_LSR" (see Sect. 4 of Könyves et al. 2015 for details).
|
| Note: The sizes listed in Col. 5 and Col. 6 provide estimates of the object's outer radius when the core can be approximately described by a Gaussian distribution, as is the case for a critical Bonnor-Ebert spheroid. 
|       In the case of a protostellar core with pronounced power-law wings, however, the quoted sizes may significantly underestimate the true outer radius.
